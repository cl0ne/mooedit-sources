Introduction

When compiled with Python support, medit uses PyGtk. Its functionality is exposed through moo module. You can use built-in Python console available from Tools menu to experiment with medit API.

Note

Classes and functions documented here are guaranteed to work as long as you follow the rules from their documentation (most often there are no special rules, but for some functions you may or may not use named arguments, etc.)

Warning

moo module has more classes and functions than documented here, but undocumented classes and functions may or may not work differently in the future and they may disappear without notice. Contact the author if you need functions which are not present here.
